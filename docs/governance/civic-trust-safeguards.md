# Civic Trust Safeguards

Status: Initial public trust baseline.

## Required Safeguards

- Transparency coverage: residents and responders understand what the platform does and does not do.
- Operator accountability: operator decisions are logged, reviewable, and attributable.
- Appeal mechanism: affected users can request review of trust, suspension, closure, or misconduct decisions.
- Misuse reporting: residents, responders, and institutions can report abuse or coercion.
- Audit visibility: governance reviewers can inspect incident, evidence, and override trails.
- Privacy minimization: sensitive data is collected only for active coordination and lawful audit need.
- Anti-surveillance boundary: infrastructure analytics must not become identity surveillance, predictive policing, or mass tracking.

## Trust Boundary Statement

The platform coordinates help. It does not replace police, clinics, courts, regulators, emergency agencies, or community governance. It must represent uncertainty honestly and avoid implying guarantees it cannot operationally provide.

## Citizen Protection Rules

- False alarm review must distinguish confusion, distress, coercion, and malicious abuse.
- Trust penalties require reviewable evidence.
- Sensitive location exposure must stay inside active incident need.
- Appeals must not erase audit records.
- Public reporting must aggregate safety and reliability, not expose personal incident details.
