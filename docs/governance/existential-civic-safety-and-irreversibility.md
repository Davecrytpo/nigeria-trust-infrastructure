# Existential Civic Safety and Irreversibility Constraints

Status: Non-certifying civilizational safety control.

## Purpose

Existential civic safety modeling ensures the platform strengthens society without becoming societally indispensable, irreversible, monopolistic, or civilization-scale coupled. The platform must remain removable without causing civic collapse.

## Irreversibility Risk

Monitor whether the platform creates:

- Societal dependency accumulation.
- Institutional lock-in.
- Coordination monoculture.
- Operational irreversibility.
- Infrastructure replacement impossibility.
- Over-centralized emergency reliance.

## Civic Self-Sufficiency

The platform must preserve:

- Local initiative.
- Decentralized coordination.
- Offline community capability.
- Independent institutional competence.
- Non-platform emergency pathways.
- Manual fallback governance.

## Failure Survivability

The platform must fail safely during:

- Catastrophic infrastructure shutdown.
- Telecom collapse.
- Governance collapse.
- Institutional withdrawal.
- Cloud provider disappearance.
- Operator abandonment.
- Public trust collapse.

## Anti-Monopoly Coordination

The platform must resist:

- Single-system emergency dependence.
- Coordination monopolization.
- Institutional over-centralization.
- Platform-exclusive workflows.
- Governance single points of failure.

Plurality is a safety property, not an inefficiency.

## Reversible Infrastructure

Deployment must preserve:

- Graceful shutdown pathways.
- Regional decoupling.
- Partial retirement capability.
- Controlled decommissioning.
- Community transition plans.
- Fallback operational continuity.

## Civilizational Coupling

The platform should detect when institutions, responders, governance assumptions, or emergency culture become too deeply platform-coupled to remain autonomous.

## Redundant Societal Capability

The platform should preserve independent emergency procedures, non-digital coordination norms, community preparedness, institutional diversity, distributed governance, and manual operational competence.

## Power Limits

Existential safety requires constrained authority, bounded operational scope, removable governance layers, independent auditability, reversible deployment, and distributed legitimacy.

## Post-Infrastructure Survivability

The platform must continuously ask whether society could transition away from it safely while preserving community resilience, institutional competence, governance continuity, and public trust.

## Decision States

- `existential-safety-freeze`: dependency, coupling, monopoly, unsafe failure, or non-removability is unacceptable.
- `restrict-and-restore-societal-self-sufficiency`: operation must be constrained while independent civic capability is rebuilt.
- `heightened-irreversibility-watch`: dependency pressure requires elevated monitoring.
- `continue-removable-resilience-support`: operation may continue while preserving removability and distributed resilience.

## Non-Certifying Boundary

Existential safety limits irreversibility. It does not certify readiness, authorize expansion, or override failed readiness, stress, convergence, governance, telecom, trust, or field survivability gates.
