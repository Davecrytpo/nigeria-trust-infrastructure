# Civic Sustainability and Long-Horizon Survivability

Status: Sustainability risk baseline.

## Purpose

Civic sustainability evaluates whether the platform can remain socially, economically, operationally, and institutionally survivable over long periods of civic pressure.

## Sustainability Domains

- Maintenance continuity.
- Organizational succession.
- Staffing durability.
- Infrastructure aging.
- Operational decay.
- Documentation continuity.

## Economic Survivability

- Telecom operating costs.
- SMS cost expansion.
- Infrastructure scaling costs.
- Responder support burden.
- Institutional funding volatility.
- Civic dependency burden.
- Maintenance affordability.

## Human and Governance Fatigue

- Chronic operational exhaustion.
- Governance review fatigue.
- Institutional complacency.
- Oversight degradation.
- Shortcut normalization.
- Procedural abandonment.

## Community Trust Evolution

- Trust accumulation.
- Trust erosion.
- Expectation inflation.
- Disappointment memory.
- Rumor propagation.
- Perceived bias.
- Fear amplification.
- Civic legitimacy drift.

## Dependency Fragility

- Telecom provider dependency.
- Cloud provider dependency.
- Institutional partnership fragility.
- SMS gateway concentration.
- Regional infrastructure asymmetry.
- Operator turnover risk.

## Governance Renewal

- Renewable oversight.
- Governance succession continuity.
- Rotating accountability.
- Institutional memory preservation.
- Audit continuity.
- Distributed review durability.

## Infrastructure Minimalism

The platform should continuously resist unnecessary feature growth, governance surface expansion, hidden operational debt, and maintenance burden accumulation.

## Decision States

- `sustainability-freeze`: operation is socially, economically, institutionally, or operationally unsustainable.
- `restrict-and-rebuild-sustainability`: reduce load and rebuild the long-term operating base.
- `heightened-sustainability-watch`: monitor and remediate early sustainability risk.
- `continue-sustainable-limited-operation`: limited operation may continue without increasing authority.

## Non-Certifying Boundary

Sustainability supports long-horizon survivability. It does not certify readiness, justify expansion, or override failed survivability, stress, or convergence gates.
