# Epistemic Resilience and Decision Humility

Status: Decision-humility control baseline.

## Purpose

Epistemic resilience prevents operational memory from becoming false certainty. The platform must remain cautious even when historical patterns appear stable.

## Humility Signals

- Incomplete sample coverage.
- Missing independent sources.
- Stale observations.
- Contradictory field evidence.
- Model error recurrence.
- Unexplained operational incidents.
- Hidden anomalies beneath positive stability trends.

## Memory Aging

Historical observations decay with time. Older evidence loses authority unless recent recurrence and independent validation support it. Contradictions reduce confidence immediately.

## Overconfidence Detection

The system flags:

- Repeated patterns without independent validation.
- Recent contradictions hidden beneath high historical confidence.
- Chronic risk being treated as normal.
- Operational memory being misused as certification evidence.
- Regional familiarity reducing governance review discipline.
- Positive stability trends hiding unresolved anomalies.

## Multi-Perspective Truth

Operational truth should compare operator, responder, telemetry, replay, governance, and telecom perspectives. Missing or conflicting sources reduce confidence and force corroboration before action.

## Decision States

- `freeze-certainty-claims`: certainty is unsafe; reopen review and block readiness narratives.
- `restrict-to-uncertainty-first-decisions`: decisions must explicitly list unknowns, assumptions, and rollback paths.
- `allow-conditional-supervised-decisions`: decisions may proceed only with supervision and conditional language.
- `continue-controlled-observation`: observation may continue without increasing operational authority.

## Non-Certifying Boundary

Epistemic resilience can only reduce certainty or maintain caution. It cannot certify readiness, override survivability failures, or replace live operational truth.
