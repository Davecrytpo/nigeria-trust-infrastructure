# Operational Reality Accumulation

Status: Longitudinal evidence baseline.

## Purpose

Operational reality accumulation records how civic coordination actually behaves over time. It exists to make the platform more historically informed and less assumption-driven. It does not certify deployment readiness.

## Reality Records

Each operational record should capture:

- Telecom state, provider, delayed receipts, packet instability, and degraded-mode hours.
- Responder abandonment, acknowledgment delay, reliability trend, and trust volatility.
- Operator fatigue, escalation inconsistency, hesitation, and overload signals.
- Synchronization drift, replay anomalies, and prolonged recovery quality.
- Governance review completion, missed oversight, policy drift, and supervisor overload.
- Institutional response, handoff failures, non-response, and authority ambiguity.

## Memory Outputs

- Recurrence rates for telecom instability, responder abandonment, operator fatigue, escalation inconsistency, sync drift, trust volatility, and governance misses.
- Chronic instability fingerprints for regions, telecom providers, and institutions.
- Reality-vs-model drift showing where simulated expectations no longer match observed outcomes.
- False-normalization indicators showing where degraded conditions are being described as normal.

## Governance Use

Operational memory should make decisions more cautious when uncertainty increases. It should support restricted learning, freezes, shadow-only validation, or deeper review when history shows repeated instability.

## Non-Override Boundary

Historical memory may justify greater caution. It must not override failed `field:test`, `field:stress`, or `field:convergence` gates. A mature memory system can explain why the platform should remain restricted; it cannot declare readiness by itself.
