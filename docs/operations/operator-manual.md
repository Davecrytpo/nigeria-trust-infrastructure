# Operator Manual

Status: Operational baseline for controlled pilot use.

## Operator Mission

Operators preserve clarity during emergencies. They validate incident state, assign ownership, escalate when response is weak, and maintain a truthful audit trail. Operators do not improvise undocumented enforcement actions.

## Shift Start Checklist

- Confirm active pilot zone, shift supervisor, and institutional escalation contacts.
- Confirm SMS provider status, API status, queue health, and incident dashboard access.
- Confirm current responder roster, unavailable responders, suspended responders, and clinic or police contacts.
- Review unresolved incidents and handoff notes from previous shift.

## Incident Handling Standard

1. Confirm incident category, location confidence, requester contact path, and current stage.
2. Check duplicate or related incidents before creating parallel coordination.
3. Assign verified responders only inside approved territory and approved incident categories.
4. Escalate if no responder acknowledges within the active severity threshold.
5. Record every manual override, escalation, reassignment, and closure reason.
6. Close only when resolution evidence, operator notes, or supervisor approval is present.

## Escalation Thresholds

- Critical life-safety incidents escalate immediately to supervisor and institutional node.
- No responder acknowledgment within 3 minutes escalates to operator supervisor.
- GPS drift above 150 meters requires location confirmation by call, SMS, landmark, or trusted witness.
- Repeated false or abusive alerts require trust review, not automatic punishment.
- Any responder misconduct allegation freezes noncritical assignments pending review.

## Prohibited Operator Actions

- Do not expose requester location beyond active incident need.
- Do not assign unverified volunteers to high-risk incidents.
- Do not delete incident evidence or audit records.
- Do not use urban intelligence outputs for surveillance, profiling, or enforcement.
- Do not close unresolved incidents to improve metrics.
