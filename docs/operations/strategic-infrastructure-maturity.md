# Strategic Infrastructure Maturity

Status: Initial long-horizon maturity baseline.

## Purpose

Strategic maturity evaluates whether the platform can preserve operational trust over weeks of instability, not only survive isolated incidents. It treats trust, institutions, regions, responders, operators, and telecom as evolving ecosystem variables.

## Long-Horizon Resilience Signals

- Multi-week telecom degradation.
- Persistent operator overload.
- Repeated disaster-mode activation.
- Responder dropout and fatigue.
- Chronic replay divergence.
- Institutional acknowledgment drift.

## Operational Trust Durability

Trust durability is longitudinal. It degrades when responder reliability, operator confidence, institutional participation, false-report pressure, chronic instability zones, or trust volatility worsen over time.

Durability actions:

- `maintain-monitoring`: trust remains durable.
- `increase-review-cadence`: trust is strained and needs closer review.
- `restrict-expansion-and-rebuild-trust`: trust is fragile; do not expand.
- `pause-expansion-and-governance-review`: trust is eroding; freeze growth pressure.

## Institutional Realism

Institutions are not binary partners. They are scored by acknowledgment, handoff completion, communication continuity, authority clarity, overload, and professionalism incidents.

Integration modes:

- `direct-handoff`: institution is strong enough for normal handoff.
- `supervised-handoff`: institution can participate with operator oversight.
- `fallback-only`: institution is weak and should not be primary.
- `do-not-depend`: institution is unreliable for operational planning.

## Regional Deployment Maturity

Regional readiness is evaluated by telecom reliability, institutional capacity, responder density, operator coverage, governance readiness, and degradation diversity.

Recommended regional modes:

- `controlled-live-pilot`.
- `limited-hours-supervised-pilot`.
- `shadow-mode-and-drills`.
- `governance-and-capacity-building`.

No region should be expanded because another region is performing well. Regional operational diversity must be respected.

## Governance Posture

Governance posture combines chronic operational risk, trust durability, weakest institution reliability, and audit backlog.

Postures:

- `maintain-controlled-pilot`.
- `supervised-growth`.
- `restricted-expansion`.
- `freeze-expansion`.

This is intentionally conservative. Governance maturity is part of infrastructure reliability.
