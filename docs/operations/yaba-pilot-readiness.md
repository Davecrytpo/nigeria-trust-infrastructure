# Yaba Pilot Readiness

Status: Controlled deployment preparation baseline.

## Pilot Scope

Yaba is the first operational coordination zone. The pilot must start with a narrow area, limited hours, named operators, verified responders, and clear incident categories. Expansion requires evidence that reliability, trust, and human coordination are stable.

## Readiness Gates

- Operator team trained on manual, escalation protocol, audit rules, and closure standards.
- Responder cohort verified, territory-limited, trained, and scheduled.
- Institutional contacts confirmed for clinic, police, estate, school, or community nodes.
- Telecom monitoring active for app delivery, SMS delivery, callback delay, and provider outage.
- Field drills pass for weak network, low battery, delayed SMS, GPS drift, and multi-incident surge.
- Incident review board established before live pilot incidents begin.

## Staffing Model

- Pilot operator: monitors queue, validates incidents, assigns responders, and records decisions.
- Shift supervisor: owns escalations, disputed closures, and institutional handoffs.
- Trust administrator: manages responder verification, suspension, and reinstatement.
- Field coordinator: handles community onboarding, responder attendance, and local feedback.
- Institutional liaison: maintains clinic, police, and other formal partner contact paths.

## Pilot Metrics

- Acknowledgment latency by severity and channel.
- Responder acceptance and no-response rates.
- Offline recovery success after reconnection.
- Duplicate and false positive incident rates.
- Operator escalation load per shift.
- Closure quality and audit completeness.
- Community trust signals from post-incident review.

## Rollout Safety

Start with dry runs, then shadow mode, then limited live hours. Do not run full public launch until responders, operators, institutions, and telecom failure paths have all been validated under field conditions.
