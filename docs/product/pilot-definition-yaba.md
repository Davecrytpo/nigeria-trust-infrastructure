# Pilot Definition - Yaba

Status: Working draft based on partial source material.

## Pilot Objective

Prove that a tightly scoped emergency coordination platform can improve trusted local response in one defined neighborhood before expanding.

## Working Location Assumption

Use Yaba, Lagos as the first launch area because it is the only location named in the readable source.

## Recommended Pilot Shape

Do not launch across all of Yaba at once.

Start with:

- one neighborhood or district
- one operator team
- one small responder network
- one clear set of incident types

## Controlled Field Limits

- maximum 300 residents
- maximum 25 verified responders
- minimum 2 active or on-call operators
- maximum 8 operating hours per pilot day
- one neighborhood only
- no high-risk category without supervisor-only workflow
- no live operation without degraded-state, telecom, replay, trust, institutional, escalation, and recovery visibility

## Selection Criteria For The First Neighborhood

- mixed residential and commercial activity
- strong community or estate leadership
- reachable responder density
- mobile-data coverage good enough for live use
- local partners willing to participate

## Planning Assumptions

- 200 to 500 pilot residents
- 20 to 40 verified responders
- 2 to 4 operators or trust staff
- active incident coverage during defined pilot hours

These are starting assumptions only and should be validated.

## First Pilot Metrics

### First 30 Days

- onboard first responder cohort
- onboard first resident cohort
- test alert creation end to end
- measure acknowledgment speed

### First 60 Days

- reduce alert creation friction
- improve responder acceptance rates
- reduce false or duplicate incidents

### First 90 Days

- confirm whether the model is trusted enough to scale
- decide whether to expand to a second neighborhood
- identify the highest-value product and operational upgrades

## Go or No-Go Questions

- Are residents actually using the emergency flow?
- Are verified responders reacting fast enough?
- Is manual coordination operationally manageable?
- Is the trust model strong enough to avoid abuse?
- Can the pilot be paused or frozen immediately when safety constraints trigger?
- Can operators see degradation and convergence state in real time?
- Are telecom limitations validated with real devices and real providers?
